GETTING STARTED

Using the Frame

The Frame, which is accessible from any view, contains peripheral information that is used across all views, incoming invitations and notifications, a clipboard, and buddies, and open activities.
In order to reveal the Frame, take the mouse to any one of the corners or use the shortcut Alt-Shift-F
In order to change the settings and sensitivity of revealing the Frame, kindly check out the last section.

Once the Frame is revealed, you will see a lot of icons in it. Amongst the top, you will find the options to 1. Go to Neighborhood View (F1) - Used to interact with people Worldwide 2. Group View (F2) - Used to interact with people locally 3. Home (F3) - Used to check the contents of your system only 4. Activity (F4) 5. Journal (F5) You can also find a lot of other options in the Frame. You can explore these by clicking the respective icons.

Using Journal

From the Frame, Click on the Journal button.

This will open up the Journal.
You can think of this as call history, but a much more sophisticated, usable and powerful. It logs all the times you open an activity and what you do inside it. You can click on any instance and add/view/edit the description.

To see the Journal Entry, or further details of the entry you see in the list, kindly press the play-like button, as marked in red in the previous photo.
You will see the entry now.
In this window, you can add/edit various fields like the Title, Description, Tags and Comments
Note: The colored text written in this image is just for your reference and not a part of what you would see

Also you might at times need to select a few items in the Journal's list and maybe remove it, or copy/export them.
In order to do that, you can use the checkboxes shown to the right of each item. Checking the items you want to change, you will see a different set of icons in the top panel, which you can now use to do what you desire to the group of selected entries.

Connect to a Wi-Fi

Take the mouse to one of the corners, in order to reveal the Frame.

Click on the Neighborhood button from the Frame.

Search for the Wi-Fi SSID from amongst all the circles shown.
Click on the Wi-Fi and select Connect.

If it is the first time use, it will ask for the Wi-Fi password.
Entering that will connect you to the Wi-Fi successfully.

Changing Frame Settings

On home, click the XO Icon, and choose My Settings.

In the Settings Window, select Frame Icon.

In the Activation Delay, you can choose the delay it takes to show the Frame from the edges as well as corners.
